Andhra pradesh: Kuchipudi

Kuchipudi is a classical Indian dance style that originated in the village of Kuchipudi in Andhra Pradesh. It is known for its graceful movements, expressive facial expressions, and elaborate costumes. Kuchipudi is a dance-drama form, which means that it tells stories through dance, music, and song. Kuchipudi is one of the classical styles of Indian dance. Around the third and fourth decade of this century it emerged out of a long rich tradition of dance-drama of the same name. In fact, Kuchipudi is the name of a village in the Krishna district of Andhra Pradesh. It is about 35 km. from Vijayawada. Music: Kuchipudi is performed to classical Carnatic music. A typical orchestra for a Kuchipudi recital includes the mridangam, flute, and violin Themes: The themes are mostly derived from the scriptures and mythology. Tarangam: A unique feature of Kuchipudi is the Tarangam, in which the performer dances on the edges of a brass plate. Regional banis: Kuchipudi has several regional banis (styles) that developed because of the uniqueness and creativity of gurus (teachers). Performance: The dance performance begins with the sprinkling of holy water and the burning of incense.
